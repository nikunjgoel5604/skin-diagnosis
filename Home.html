Preliminary Diagnosis Of Dermatological Manifestations
Skin Diagnosis

Internal Disease

These are skin-related symptoms or conditions that are indicative of underlying internal health issues or systemic diseases.

Examples include petechiae (tiny red or purple spots on the skin, which can be a sign of bleeding disorders), jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes, often associated with liver problems), and skin changes related to autoimmune diseases like lupus.

These manifestations often require medical evaluation and diagnostic tests to identify and address the underlying cause, as the skin changes may be secondary to internal problems.

Skin Diagnosis

External Disease

These are skin-related symptoms or conditions that are visible on the surface of the skin.

Examples include rashes, hives, acne, psoriasis, eczema, dermatitis, warts, and skin infections.

These manifestations are typically noticeable without the need for specialized diagnostic tools or tests.
